## 🚀 1965. Employees With Missing Information

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to find employees who have **missing information**.

An employee is considered to have missing information if:

- They exist in the **Employees** table but not in the **Salaries** table, or
- They exist in the **Salaries** table but not in the **Employees** table.

We can identify both cases using two `LEFT JOIN`s and combine the results with `UNION`.

---

## 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT e.employee_id
FROM Employees e
LEFT JOIN Salaries s
ON e.employee_id = s.employee_id
WHERE s.employee_id IS NULL
UNION
SELECT s.employee_id
FROM Salaries s
LEFT JOIN Employees e
ON s.employee_id = e.employee_id
WHERE e.employee_id IS NULL

ORDER BY employee_id;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Perform a `LEFT JOIN` from `Employees` to `Salaries`.
2. Keep employees whose salary record is missing (`s.employee_id IS NULL`).
3. Perform another `LEFT JOIN` from `Salaries` to `Employees`.
4. Keep salary records whose employee information is missing (`e.employee_id IS NULL`).
5. Combine both result sets using `UNION`.
6. Sort the final result by `employee_id`.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n + m)`

*(`n` = number of employees, `m` = number of salary records)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n + m)`
