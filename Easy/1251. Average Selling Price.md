## 🚀 1251. Average Selling Price

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to calculate the **average selling price** for each product.

For every unit sold, we multiply the **price** by the **number of units** sold to get the total revenue. Then, we divide the total revenue by the total number of units sold.

Since some products may have **no units sold**, we use a `LEFT JOIN` to include all products and return an average price of **0** for those products.

---

## 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT
    p.product_id,
    IFNULL(
        ROUND(
            SUM(p.price * u.units) / SUM(u.units),
            2
        ),
        0
    ) AS average_price
FROM Prices p
LEFT JOIN UnitsSold u
ON p.product_id = u.product_id
AND u.purchase_date BETWEEN p.start_date AND p.end_date
GROUP BY p.product_id;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Join the `Prices` and `UnitsSold` tables using `product_id`.
2. Match each sale with the correct price by checking whether the `purchase_date` falls between the price's `start_date` and `end_date`.
3. Calculate the total revenue using:
   - `SUM(price * units)`
4. Calculate the total number of units sold using:
   - `SUM(units)`
5. Divide the total revenue by the total units sold to obtain the weighted average selling price.
6. Round the result to **2 decimal places**.
7. Use `IFNULL()` to return `0` for products with no sales.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(single join followed by grouping)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(stores grouped products)*
